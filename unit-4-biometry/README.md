## ğŸ›ï¸ The 8 Distribution Filters

Think of these as different "settings" on your statistical stethoscope.

### 1ï¸âƒ£ Binomial Distribution - *Success/Failure Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸŒ± Out of 20 seeds planted, how many germinate?
- **Pattern**: Fixed trials, yes/no outcomes
- **Parameters**: n (trials), p (probability)

### 2ï¸âƒ£ Poisson Distribution - *Rare Events Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸ¦‹ New species colonizing per year
- **Pattern**: Rare events in fixed intervals
- **Parameters**: Î» (rate)

### 3ï¸âƒ£ Normal Distribution - *Natural Variation Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸ“ Plant heights in population
- **Pattern**: Continuous, symmetric variation
- **Parameters**: Î¼ (mean), Ïƒ (standard deviation)

### 4ï¸âƒ£ Uniform Distribution - *Equal Probability Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸ² Random position on transect
- **Pattern**: All outcomes equally likely
- **Parameters**: a (min), b (max)

### 5ï¸âƒ£ Exponential Distribution - *Waiting Time Filter*
- **Example**: â±ï¸ Time between births
- **Pattern**: Memoryless waiting times
- **Parameters**: Î» (rate)

### 6ï¸âƒ£ Geometric Distribution - *Trials to Success Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸ¯ Seeds planted until one germinates
- **Pattern**: Number of trials until first success
- **Parameters**: p (probability)

### 7ï¸âƒ£ Negative Binomial Distribution - *Overdispersed Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸ¦  Parasites per host
- **Pattern**: Clumped/aggregated distributions
- **Parameters**: r (successes), p (probability)

### 8ï¸âƒ£ Log-Normal Distribution - *Multiplicative Filter*
- **Example**: ğŸ“Š Population sizes across species
- **Pattern**: Right-skewed, positive values only
- **Parameters**: Î¼ (log mean), Ïƒ (log SD)
